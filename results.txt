Insight: This is an incredibly weak correlation! It strongly supports the hypothesis that the NIRF Graduation Outcomes (GO) metric is overweighting the *quantity* of placements rather than the *quality* (salary/LPA) of the jobs.

--- Difference-in-Differences (DiD) Analysis (Karnataka vs Tamil Nadu) ---
*Note: COVID-19 years (2020 and 2021) were explicitly excluded.*

1. Inclusivity & Equity in STEMM (OI Score)
  - Karnataka Change (Pre vs Post NEP): +2.71
  - Tamil Nadu Change (Pre vs Post NEP): -0.74
  - DiD Estimator (Treatment Effect): +3.45
  - Insight: Massive Success! The NEP's inclusivity mandates significantly boosted Karnataka's Outreach & Inclusivity compared to Tamil Nadu.

2. Digital Divide & Infrastructure Funding (TLR Score)
  - Karnataka Change (Pre vs Post NEP): +4.81
  - Tamil Nadu Change (Pre vs Post NEP): +5.37
  - DiD Estimator (Treatment Effect): -0.56
  - Insight: Both states improved infrastructure heavily, but Tamil Nadu outpaced Karnataka slightly. The NEP did not result in an outsized infrastructure advantage.

3. Institutional Restructuring & Autonomy (RPC Score)
  - Karnataka Change (Pre vs Post NEP): +4.95
  - Tamil Nadu Change (Pre vs Post NEP): +6.09
  - DiD Estimator (Treatment Effect): -1.14
  - Insight: The NEP's goal to build "multidisciplinary research clusters" experienced friction; Tamil Nadu actually scaled its research output faster than Karnataka over the same period.
